Tim Manley

About Me

Hi! I'm Tim, a Software Engineer, Singer and Student at Princeton University. I love to solve problems, whether that be a well hidden bug, a crossword, a math problem, or vocal arrangement - nothing is off limits!

Education
I grew up in the UK, where I went to high school at Eton College, getting top grades in 11 GCSEs, and 4 A*s in my A-Levels (Computer Science, Math, Further Math, and Physics). I then decided to come to the USA to study Computer Science at Princeton University, whilst also continuing my interest in Math by getting a minor in Applied and Computational Math.
Coding
I have been coding since the age of 13 when I was first introduced to Python in school. Ever since, I've loved learning new techniques, languages and technologies as well as deepening my core Computer Science knowledge. My main interests lie in Software Development, Machine Learning, and Research.

I have the most experience working with Python and SQL specifically alongside Flask and PostgreSQL, but I am also familiar with Java, C, and Swift as well as HTML/CSS and JQuery.

Right now, I'm working on improving my frontend skills, particularly with Bootstrap5 and JavaScript, and I am looking into exploring more advanced frameworks such as React.
Singing
I first started singing when I was 3 years old, when my piano teacher suggested she give me singing lessons as well. I then joined the Choir of King's College Cambridge as a treble when I was 7, singing on the TV, radio and around the world, in addition to the six weekly services in the chapel. I then went to Eton where I sang as a Bass in the Chapel Choir, as well as in the school's acapella group The Incognitos. Currently, I'm in the Princeton University Glee Club and Chamber Choir and I'm also the Music Director of Princeton's oldest acapella group, The Princeton Nassoons, and the treasurer of Decem, a 10 person vocal consort.
